# Vedic — Rahu Kalam timing and Nakshatra mood

Vedic Jyotish has many components; for fortune-telling-skill purposes, two are the most useful and computable.

## Rahu Kalam — daily inauspicious window

A 1.5-hour daily window when starting important things is traditionally avoided. Varies by weekday. The exact time is sunrise-relative, so the windows below are **approximations for Bangkok** — for other longitudes/latitudes, shift by the local sunrise offset.

| Weekday | Rahu Kalam (Bangkok approx.) |
|---|---|
| Sunday | 16:30–18:00 |
| Monday | 07:30–09:00 |
| Tuesday | 15:00–16:30 |
| Wednesday | 12:00–13:30 |
| Thursday | 13:30–15:00 |
| Friday | 10:30–12:00 |
| Saturday | 09:00–10:30 |

Avoid starting important things during Rahu Kalam — signing, launching, asking, beginning. Routine work and finishing things is fine.

## Nakshatra flavor — the Moon's daily lunar mansion

The Moon's nakshatra sets the day's emotional weather. Computing the exact nakshatra needs an ephemeris — if uncertain, omit and use other signals.

When you can identify today's nakshatra, the flavor categories are:

**Soft / nourishing** — good for beginnings, gentleness, healing, study
- Rohini, Mrigashira, Pushya, Hasta, Chitra, Anuradha, Revati

**Sharp / fierce** — good for endings, bold action, breaking through, confrontation
- Bharani, Magha, Purva Phalguni, Jyeshtha, Mula, Purva Ashadha

**Mixed / movable** — good for travel, change, transitions
- Punarvasu, Swati, Shravana, Dhanishta, Shatabhisha

## How to use

For "should I do X today?", Rahu Kalam gives a hard hour-window to avoid. Nakshatra (when known) gives the day's emotional cast — match the question to the flavor category.

## When to skip

If neither the local sunrise time nor today's nakshatra can be confirmed, drop Vedic from this reading and lean on Thai or Chinese instead. Don't fabricate nakshatra values.
