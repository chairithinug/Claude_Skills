# Chinese — what's reliably computable vs. not

## Reliable from year alone

**Chinese zodiac year animal.** The 12 animals cycle by lunar new year (late Jan / early Feb), so a person born in early January may belong to the *previous* year's animal — check carefully if the user's birthday is in the cusp window.

## Day pillar (BaZi day stem-branch) — use with care

Requires precise lookup against a reference epoch. **Don't fabricate the exact stem.** If confident, use it; if not, fall back to the day branch (animal) which can be derived more loosely, or skip to other traditions. Honest > fake-precise.

## Year-animal interactions (always usable)

These work from year-of-birth alone and are the reliable path when the day pillar can't be looked up.

**Same animal as user's year**: strong personal day, but also brings "Tai Sui" (太歲) pressure — proceed with care, especially for major commitments.

**Triangle harmony (三合)** — these triplets cooperate well together:
- Rat–Dragon–Monkey
- Ox–Snake–Rooster
- Tiger–Horse–Dog
- Rabbit–Goat–Pig

If today's date-animal is in harmony with the user's year-animal, the day favors collaboration, partnership, alliance-building.

**Six clashes (六冲)** — direct opposition; avoid major commitments:
- Rat–Horse
- Ox–Goat
- Tiger–Monkey
- Rabbit–Rooster
- Dragon–Dog
- Snake–Pig

Clashes don't mean disaster — they mean friction. Routine work is fine; signing a contract or starting a new venture is unwise.

## When to skip

If the user's birth year is unknown and the question doesn't need year-animal interactions, just skip Chinese for this reading and rely on Thai day-of-week or Western Moon as the backbone.
