# C-Suite Lens Definitions

Detailed definitions and guiding questions for each executive lens.
Load this file when you need depth on a specific lens.

---

## CEO — Strategy & Optionality
*What does the person setting long-term direction care about?*

- Does this move align with or undermine the core mission?
- What future options does this open or close?
- What does this crowd out — capital, attention, time?
- Is this the right problem to be working on right now?

---

## CFO — Capital & Financial Risk
*What does the person responsible for money and survival care about?*

- What does this cost, and what does it return?
- What are the unit economics at scale?
- What is the cash flow impact and timeline?
- What financial risks does this introduce or remove?
- Is there a path to sustainability, or does this require indefinite subsidy?

---

## COO — Execution & Operations
*What does the person responsible for making things actually work care about?*

- Can this actually be built and run with current capacity?
- What operational dependencies does this create?
- What breaks first under load?
- Is the process repeatable and scalable, or dependent on heroics?

---

## CMO — Market & Brand
*What does the person responsible for customers and perception care about?*

- How does this land with the target customer?
- What does it do to positioning relative to competitors?
- What distribution channels does it open or require?
- What reputational risk does it carry?

---

## CTO — Technology & Architecture
*What does the person responsible for technical systems care about?*

- Is this technically feasible with current or accessible resources?
- What architectural trade-offs does this introduce?
- Build vs. buy — which is smarter here?
- What technical debt does this create or inherit?
- What are the security, privacy, and compliance implications?

---

## CHRO — People & Culture
*What does the person responsible for humans and org health care about?*

- Does this require skills or capacity the team doesn't have?
- What does this do to culture, morale, or founder wellbeing?
- Is the org design right for this, or does it need to change?
- What talent risks does this introduce?
